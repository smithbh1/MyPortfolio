Santaquin: Home of the Orchard Days

Originally founded in 1851, Santaquin was originally named "Summit City" and was settled by pioneers. It was renamed to "Santaquin" in 1856 in honor of a friendy, local native chieftan. Santaquin is also know for its famous "Orchard Days", a celebration of local farmers and fruit-growers. They celebrate in the form of parades, rodeos, carnivals, scavenger hunts, and much more!


As of 2019 there was an estimate 12,865 residents with ~42% of those residents being under the age of 18. The median annual income per household was $45,323 and the per capita income was $13,725.

Activities

While Santaquin may be a small town, we always have something fun to do! We are home to over 5 public parks with baseball fields, tennis courts, basketball courts, and playgrounds. If anyone has active kids, this is the place for you! We host donations to the local foodbank as well, so we are always looking to serve and help our community prosper.
